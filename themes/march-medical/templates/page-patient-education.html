FAQs

Get quick answers to common healthcare questions and concerns. If you don't find what you need here, please don't hesitate to contact our office.

What is family medicine?

Family medicine is a comprehensive specialty that provides continuous care for individuals and families across all ages, genders, and health conditions. Our family physicians are trained to treat a wide range of medical issues and coordinate specialized care when needed.

How do I schedule an appointment?

You can schedule an appointment by calling our office during business hours or using our online scheduling system through the patient portal. For urgent matters, please call our office directly to speak with our staff.

Do you accept my insurance?

We accept most major insurance plans. Please contact our office with your insurance information, and we'll verify your coverage and benefits before your appointment. Our staff is happy to help answer any insurance-related questions.

What should I bring to my first appointment?

Please bring a valid photo ID, your insurance card, a list of current medications, any relevant medical records, and a form of payment for any copays or deductibles. If you have specific health concerns, it's helpful to write them down beforehand.

How can I access my test results?

Test results are typically available through our patient portal within 24-48 hours. You'll receive a notification when results are ready to view. If you have questions about your results, you can send a secure message to your physician through the portal or call our office.

What is your cancellation policy?

We request at least 24 hours notice for appointment cancellations or rescheduling. This allows us to offer the appointment time to other patients who may need care. Late cancellations or missed appointments may result in a fee.
